GoldMine Agencies. Eagle Web Commerce. CryptoGridX. These platforms charged Kenyan youth KSh 500–5,000 in registration fees, promised daily income, and either never paid or collapsed with everyone's money. TrueEarn was built because of them.

The Problem

The system is built to take money, not make it for you

Every year, hundreds of thousands of Kenyan youth enter a digital economy with no entry point. Scammers fill that gap.

Pay to join, then wait forever

Platforms charge KSh 500 to KSh 5,000 as "activation fees." After that, jobs are either unavailable, require more upgrades, or simply never come.

Recruit or earn nothing

Real earnings on most platforms require recruiting friends. The task work barely pays. The referral chain is the product — and when recruitment slows, the platform collapses.

Withdrawal always blocked

Users report hitting minimum thresholds, then being asked for another fee to "activate withdrawals." The money never arrives. The platform disappears.

No legitimate alternative exists

Global platforms like Upwork and Fiverr require high skills and consistent internet access. For a Form 4 leaver with a smartphone and KSh 50 of data, they are not a realistic option.
